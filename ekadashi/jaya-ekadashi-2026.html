About Jaya Ekadashi

Jaya Ekadashi, also known as Bhoumi Ekadashi, falls in the Shukla Paksha (bright fortnight) of the Magha month. The word "Jaya" means "victory," and this Ekadashi is believed to grant victory over all material attachments, negative tendencies, and even ghostly existence.

This Ekadashi is particularly powerful for those seeking liberation from past karmic bondage and freedom from cycles of suffering. It's said that observing this Ekadashi with devotion can even liberate souls trapped in ghostly realms.

üìñ The Legendary Story of Malyavan and Pushpavati

In the heavenly realm of Indra, there lived a Gandharva named Malyavan and an Apsara (celestial nymph) named Pushpavati. They were deeply in love. Once, during a performance in Indra's court, they became so absorbed in each other that they neglected their duties.

Furious at their distraction, Indra cursed them to become Pishacha (ghosts) and live on Earth in a dark forest. For many years, they suffered in ghostly forms, enduring hunger, thirst, and extreme weather.

One Magha Shukla Ekadashi, they unknowingly observed a fast‚Äînot eating due to their miserable condition‚Äîand stayed awake all night in cold. By the merit of this accidental Ekadashi observance, they were immediately liberated from their ghostly existence and returned to their celestial forms.

Lord Vishnu named this Ekadashi "Jaya" (victory) because it grants victory over even the most terrible conditions.

How to Observe Jaya Ekadashi

Preparation (Dashami)

  • Have only one meal on the day before Ekadashi
  • Avoid tamasic foods (onion, garlic, meat)
  • Begin mental preparation for the fast

Ekadashi Day Rituals

  • Wake up before sunrise and take a bath
  • Set up Lord Vishnu's altar with yellow flowers
  • Light a ghee lamp and incense
  • Offer Tulsi leaves, fruits, and panchamrit
  • Chant "Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya" 108 times
  • Read or listen to Jaya Ekadashi Vrat Katha
  • Maintain complete fast or take only fruits/milk
  • Stay awake through the night (Jagaran) meditating on Lord Vishnu

Night Vigil (Jagaran)

Staying awake on Jaya Ekadashi night is considered especially meritorious. You can:

  • Chant Vishnu Sahasranama
  • Sing bhajans and kirtans
  • Read Bhagavad Gita or Vishnu Purana
  • Meditate on Lord Vishnu's form

Special Prayers for Liberation

If you're praying for the liberation of departed souls or ancestors, Jaya Ekadashi is particularly powerful. Offer prayers specifically requesting Lord Vishnu to liberate any family members who may be suffering in lower realms.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is Jaya Ekadashi 2026?

Jaya Ekadashi 2026 falls on Friday, February 27, 2026, during the Shukla Paksha of Magha month.

Can Jaya Ekadashi really liberate ghosts?

According to the Padma Purana, the story of Malyavan and Pushpavati demonstrates that observing Jaya Ekadashi can liberate souls from ghostly existence. This is why it's recommended to pray for departed family members on this day.

Is night vigil (Jagaran) mandatory?

While not strictly mandatory, staying awake on Jaya Ekadashi night multiplies the spiritual benefits. If you can't stay awake all night, even a few hours of devotional activities are beneficial.

Why is it also called Bhoumi Ekadashi?

It's called Bhoumi Ekadashi because Malyavan and Pushpavati spent their ghostly existence on Earth (Bhoomi). Their liberation on this Ekadashi gave it the alternate name.
