‚ú® Significance of Kamada Ekadashi

Kamada means "desire-granting" in Sanskrit. This powerful Ekadashi is believed to fulfill all righteous desires of devotees who observe it with devotion and sincerity.

üôè Special Blessing: Observing Kamada Ekadashi is said to grant the merit of performing many Ashwamedha Yagnas and liberates devotees from the cycle of birth and death.

This Ekadashi falls in the spring month of Chaitra, marking a time of new beginnings. It's particularly auspicious for:

  • Those seeking fulfillment of cherished desires
  • Starting new ventures or projects
  • Seeking liberation from past sins
  • Couples desiring harmony and happiness

üìñ The Story of Kamada Ekadashi

According to the Varaha Purana, there lived a Gandharva (celestial musician) named Lalit in the city of Ratnapura. He was married to a beautiful Apsara named Lalita, and they were deeply devoted to each other.

One day, while Lalit was singing in the court of King Pundarika, he became so absorbed in thoughts of his wife that he made mistakes in his performance. A sage present in the court cursed him to become a demon for this distraction.

Transformed into a fearsome Pishacha (demon), Lalit roamed the forests in agony. His devoted wife Lalita searched everywhere and finally consulted a sage who advised her to observe Kamada Ekadashi and transfer the merit to her husband.

üí´ Lalita observed the fast with complete devotion, and by the power of Kamada Ekadashi, Lalit was freed from the curse. He regained his celestial form, more radiant than before, and the couple was reunited in divine bliss.

üôè Kamada Ekadashi Mantra

"Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"

Chant this mantra 108 times while meditating on Lord Vishnu for maximum benefit.

üìã How to Observe Kamada Ekadashi

Day Before (Dashami)

  • Eat only one simple sattvic meal
  • Avoid tamasic foods (onion, garlic, non-veg)
  • Set your intention for the fast

On Ekadashi Day

  • Wake before sunrise and take a holy bath
  • Visit a Vishnu temple or set up home altar
  • Observe full fast or consume only fruits and milk
  • Read or listen to Vishnu Sahasranama
  • Chant "Om Namo Narayanaya" throughout the day
  • Stay awake at night if possible (jagaran)

Breaking the Fast (Parana)

  • Break fast on Dwadashi morning during Parana time
  • First offer food to Lord Vishnu
  • Donate to Brahmins or the needy
