üìñ The Story of Nirjala Ekadashi

The Brahma Vaivarta Purana tells the story of Bhima, the mighty Pandava known for his enormous appetite.

Unlike his brothers, Bhima could not fast on Ekadashis because his hunger was unbearable. He felt guilty for missing this sacred observance while the rest of his family fasted devotedly.

Troubled by this, Bhima approached the great sage Vyasa for guidance. Vyasa understood Bhima's predicament and advised him to observe just ONE Ekadashi ‚Äî but the most difficult one ‚Äî completely without food or water.

üí™ Bhima observed Nirjala Ekadashi with tremendous willpower. By enduring this single difficult fast, he gained the merit of all 24 Ekadashis. This is why Nirjala Ekadashi is also called Bhima Ekadashi or Pandava Ekadashi.

‚ö†Ô∏è Health Warning - Read Before Attempting

Do NOT attempt Nirjala (waterless) fast if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Diabetic or have blood sugar issues
  • Having heart conditions or blood pressure problems
  • Elderly (above 60) or a child (under 15)
  • Taking any regular medication
  • Feeling unwell or weak

Alternative: Observe a regular Ekadashi fast with water and fruits. Lord Vishnu appreciates your intention ‚Äî health comes first!

üìã How to Safely Observe Nirjala Ekadashi

Day Before (Dashami)

  • Hydrate well throughout the day
  • Eat a balanced, sattvic meal (not too heavy)
  • Avoid salty foods that cause dehydration
  • Sleep early and rest well

On Ekadashi Day

  • Wake before sunrise and take a bath
  • Begin fast from sunrise ‚Äî no food, no water
  • Stay in cool, comfortable places
  • Avoid strenuous physical activity
  • Spend time in prayer, meditation, reading scriptures
  • Chant "Om Namo Narayanaya" or Vishnu Sahasranama
  • If possible, stay awake through the night (jagaran)

Breaking the Fast (Dwadashi Morning)

  • Break fast immediately at Parana time
  • First drink water slowly in small sips
  • Wait 15-20 minutes, then have light food
  • Avoid heavy meals immediately
  • Donate food, water, or umbrellas to Brahmins/needy

üôè Nirjala Ekadashi Mantra

"Om Namo Bhagavate Vasudevaya"

Chant this 108 times or continuously throughout the day for divine protection during the fast.
