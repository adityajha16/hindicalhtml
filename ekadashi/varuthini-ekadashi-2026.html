üõ°Ô∏è Significance of Varuthini Ekadashi

Varuthini means "one who wards off evil" or "protector" in Sanskrit. This Ekadashi creates a divine shield around devotees, protecting them from all forms of danger and negativity.

üôè Special Blessing: Observing Varuthini Ekadashi is said to grant protection equivalent to performing thousands of pilgrimages to sacred rivers like Ganga.

This Ekadashi is particularly beneficial for:

  • Protection from accidents and mishaps
  • Warding off negative energies and evil eye
  • Overcoming fear and anxiety
  • Gaining courage and inner strength
  • Safe travels and ventures

üìñ The Story of Varuthini Ekadashi

Lord Krishna narrated the glory of Varuthini Ekadashi to King Yudhishthira in the Bhavishya Purana.

Long ago, there was a powerful king named Mandhata, famous for his righteousness and devotion. He ruled his kingdom with justice and was loved by all his subjects.

Once, a terrible drought struck his kingdom. The rivers dried up, crops failed, and people suffered. The king consulted sages who advised him to observe Varuthini Ekadashi with complete devotion.

üåßÔ∏è King Mandhata observed the fast strictly, spending the entire day in prayer and meditation. By the grace of Lord Vishnu, rain clouds gathered and the drought ended. His kingdom prospered once again, and his people were saved.
