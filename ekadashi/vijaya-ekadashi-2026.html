About Vijaya Ekadashi

Vijaya Ekadashi falls in the Krishna Paksha (dark fortnight) of the Phalguna month. The name "Vijaya" means "victory," and this Ekadashi is famed for ensuring success in all endeavors, especially those that seem impossible.

What makes this Ekadashi extraordinarily special is its connection to Lord Rama. According to the Skanda Purana, Lord Rama Himself observed this Ekadashi before launching His campaign against the demon king Ravana, ensuring His ultimate victory.

üèπ Lord Rama's Connection to Vijaya Ekadashi

When Lord Rama was preparing to build the bridge (Rama Setu) to Lanka and rescue Sita from Ravana, He sought divine guidance. The great sage Bakadalbhya advised Lord Rama to observe Vijaya Ekadashi before the military campaign.

Following the sage's counsel, Lord Rama, along with His brother Lakshmana and the entire Vanara army led by Hanuman, observed this sacred fast. After the Ekadashi, they successfully built the bridge, crossed the ocean, and achieved the legendary victory over Ravana.

This is why Vijaya Ekadashi is considered the most powerful Ekadashi for those facing seemingly impossible challenges.

üìñ The Story from Skanda Purana

Lord Brahma narrated this story to his son Narada: During the Treta Yuga, when Lord Rama was camping at the seashore with His army, wondering how to cross the vast ocean to reach Lanka, He was filled with concern.

At that time, Sage Bakadalbhya appeared and said, "O Rama, I know why You are troubled. Listen to me. There is an Ekadashi in the Krishna Paksha of Phalguna month called Vijaya. If You observe this Ekadashi with devotion, You will certainly cross this ocean and defeat the invincible Ravana."

Lord Rama followed the sage's instructions precisely. After observing Vijaya Ekadashi, the sea god Varuna appeared and provided the way for building the bridge. The rest is the glorious history of Ramayana.

When to Observe Vijaya Ekadashi

Vijaya Ekadashi is especially recommended when you are:

  • Facing a major challenge or obstacle in life
  • Starting a new business or project
  • Appearing for competitive exams or interviews
  • Dealing with legal matters or disputes
  • Facing health challenges
  • Seeking victory over enemies or competitors
  • Needing courage for any difficult task

How to Observe Vijaya Ekadashi

The Night Before (Dashami)

  • Have only one sattvic meal
  • Avoid anger, negative thoughts, and gossip
  • Sleep on the floor or a simple mat
  • Resolve firmly to observe the fast

Ekadashi Day Rituals

  • Wake up during Brahma Muhurta (before sunrise)
  • Take a bath and wear fresh clothes
  • Set up Lord Vishnu's altar with yellow flowers and Tulsi
  • Also worship Lord Rama alongside Vishnu
  • Offer fruits, panchamrit, and incense
  • Light a ghee lamp throughout the day
  • Chant Vishnu Sahasranama and Ram Naam
  • Read or listen to Vijaya Ekadashi Vrat Katha
  • Read chapters from Ramayana, especially Yuddha Kanda

Special Worship for Victory

If observing for a specific victory or challenge:

  • Write your challenge/goal on paper
  • Place it at Lord Rama's feet during worship
  • Chant "Jai Shri Ram" 108 times
  • Pray with specific intention for victory
  • Have complete faith in the Lord's grace

Spiritual Benefits of Vijaya Ekadashi

  • Guaranteed victory in righteous endeavors
  • Removal of all obstacles (Vighnanashan)
  • Courage to face impossible challenges
  • Protection from enemies and competitors
  • Success in legal and official matters
  • Blessings of Lord Rama and Hanuman
  • Merit equal to visiting all holy places
  • Liberation from past karmic obstacles

Frequently Asked Questions

When is Vijaya Ekadashi 2026?

Vijaya Ekadashi 2026 falls on Saturday, March 14, 2026, during the Krishna Paksha of Phalguna month.

Why is it called Vijaya Ekadashi?

"Vijaya" means victory. This Ekadashi guarantees success in righteous endeavors. Lord Rama observed it before defeating Ravana, hence it became known as Vijaya (Victory) Ekadashi.

Should I worship Lord Rama or Lord Vishnu?

Lord Rama is an avatar of Lord Vishnu, so worshipping either is meritorious. On Vijaya Ekadashi, it's traditional to worship both‚ÄîLord Vishnu as the Supreme Being and Lord Rama as the exemplar of this Ekadashi's power.

Can I observe this for success in exams or business?

Absolutely! Vijaya Ekadashi is observed for success in any legitimate, righteous endeavor‚Äîexams, business, legal matters, health challenges, or any obstacle you're facing.
