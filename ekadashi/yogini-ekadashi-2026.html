üìñ The Story of Yogini Ekadashi

The Brahma Vaivarta Purana narrates the story of a gardener named Hemamali who served King Kubera, the lord of wealth, in the celestial city of Alakapuri.

Hemamali's duty was to collect flowers daily for King Kubera's puja of Lord Shiva. However, Hemamali was deeply attached to his beautiful wife, Visalakshi. One day, he became so engrossed with her that he forgot to bring the flowers.

Furious, Kubera cursed Hemamali to become a leper and live on Earth, separated from his beloved wife forever.

The suffering Hemamali wandered through forests until he met the great sage Markandeya, who advised him to observe Yogini Ekadashi with complete devotion.

‚ú® Hemamali observed Yogini Ekadashi faithfully. By its divine power, his leprosy was cured, his sins were forgiven, and he was restored to his celestial position ‚Äî reunited with his beloved Visalakshi.

üôè Yogini Ekadashi Mantra

"Om Yoginyai Namah"

Chant this mantra to invoke the blessings of divine Yoginis and overcome fear.

üìã How to Observe Yogini Ekadashi

  • Wake before sunrise and take a purifying bath
  • Wear clean clothes, preferably in light colors
  • Offer prayers to Lord Vishnu with Tulsi leaves and flowers
  • Observe complete fast or consume only fruits and milk
  • Read the story of Yogini Ekadashi and Hemamali
  • Chant Vishnu Sahasranama or "Om Namo Narayanaya"
  • Meditate on overcoming fear and attachments
  • Stay awake at night if possible (jagaran)
  • Break fast the next morning during Parana time
